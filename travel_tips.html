Uses of Currency
Currency

One Sri Lankan Rupee is made up of 100 cents. Currency notes in the denominations of Rs. 10. Rs. 20, Rs. 50, Rs. 100, Rs.200, Rs.500, Rs.1000, and Rs.5000. Coins are in the following denominations: 1 cent, 2 cent, 5 cent, 10 cent, 25 cent, 50 cent, one rupee, two rupees, five rupees and ten rupees.

What to wear
What to wear
  • Light cotton dress and light weight suits.
  • A wide-brimmed beach hat and sunglasses, comfortable shoes/ sandals.
  • In the hill country a pull over will be necessary.
  • Visiting should be decently clad when visiting any place of worship.
  • Beachwear is not suitable for temples and shrines.
  • All visitors to Buddhist and Hindu temples are expected to remove footwear and hats.
  • When entering the temples shoulders and knees should be covered.
  • Hotels prefer long trousers for gents during dinner.
  • Topless sunbathing is prohibited.
Medical Matters
Medical Matters

Most of the towns have pharmacies. Should you require the service of a doctor, please contact the hotel reception and they can arrange for a doctor to call and see you. If you should suffer from tummy upset, this is often unavoidable due to changes in water, food and climate. Stick to plain dry food for a while and drink plenty of bottle water.

Swimming and Bathing
Swimming and Bathing

We do not recommend swimming in the red flag area. If the red flag is flying, this means they are strong currents and it is unsafe. Nude sunbathing is against the law here in Sri Lanka and please make sure that you cover up when entering the Reception area. Please be careful when going out in the sun for the first few days of your holiday, as the tropical sun has phenomenal burning power. Always use a high factor Sun cream and remember to drink plenty of bottled water.

Tipping
Tipping

Tipping is customary, but not obligatory. In restaurants and hotels a 10% service charge is automatically added on your bill. However, if you feel that you have had very good service, an additional tip is considered fair. You may wish to leave your hotel tipping until your departure, as most hotels often pool all tips between everyone. For guests who go on tour, you may wish to tip your driver/ guide, the amount is entirely at your discretion.

Local transport
Local transport

Travel by the public transport system is not for the faint hearted. Most of the buses and trains are overcrowded and of poor standard and maintenance, delayed and uninsured. Prices should be negotiated for short Journeys by unmetered taxis or Tuk Tuk.

Local customs
Local customs

The traditional way of greeting people is by placing your hands together and saying 'AYUBOWAN' which means 'May you have a long life' the same can be used when saying goodbye. The main faith in Sri Lanka is Buddhism. When entering any temples of places of worship, correct attire should be worn. Knees and shoulders should be covered and footwear and hats removed.

Photography
Photography

It is considered disrespectful to stand with your back turned to a Buddha statue/ picture when taking photos. It is always advisable to ask permission when taking pictures in places of worship or of local people. Many historical and holy places levy small charge for cameras and video cameras to be taken inside.

Touts
Touts

Like in most tourist destinations you will be approached by street touts or beach hawkers that offered to show you the sites or local attractions. A polite but firm "NO" will suffice to ward off their attention.

Valuables
Valuables

We recommend that all your valuable such as Money, Flight tickets, Passport, Jewellery etc., should be kept in your possession or in hotel safes at all times. Please do not leave any valuables in hotel rooms unlocked.
